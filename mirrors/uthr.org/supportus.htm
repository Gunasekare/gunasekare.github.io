Dear Friends,

In the midst of tumultuous events, in Sri Lanka and around the world, we are trying to continue our work in the coming year.  During the last few months our major accomplishments include briefings on the use of child soldiers by the LTTE, and bulletin on violations against women by state security forces.  Last year also marked the successful completion of a two and a half year book project with the publication of ‘Sri Lanka: The Arrogance of Power – Myths, Decadence & Murder,’ authored by Rajan Hoole.

 We hope you have found our work to be of value. With all the odds against us we continue this endeavor in the hope that our work will contribute to the peace and human dignity of our community. When events necessitate and when information is available, we have come out with reports and bulletins. Recently, we decided to put all of our past work on this website. This provides more detailed history and background information about UTHR (J) and the human rights situation in Sri Lanka.

Since virtually the civil society is parlayed by the politics of terror in the Tamil community our lonely voice kept the voice of many Tamils who neither want to associate with narrow ideological agenda of the LTTE  nor want condone the crimes perpetuated in their name.

The war has caused thousands of lives and massive destruction and displacement for ordinary citizens all over Sri Lanka, but particularly in the North and East. There is an historic opportunity to achieve peace with the current Norwegian mediated process. If we want to make this a sustainable peace with popular legitimacy, we need to work towards a transformative process involving civil society participation in all parts of the country. With civil-society crushed by the LTTE's authoritarian reign, the situation in the North and East is particularly fragile. In this context the work of independent voices such as the UTHR(J) will prove crucial in reclaiming the space for democratic participation in the public sphere.

At present the overwhelming desire for peace with dignity of the ordinary Tamils could not independently asserts itself beyond LTTE’s own agenda. It is imperative that the space for that accountability need to be created and expanded during this process and it in turn it should have an impact on the LTTE’s agenda. It could be only done by making the monitoring mechanism of the ceasefire more proactive and if necessary bring more independent monitoring in the war Zone. This is necessary withstanding the international pressure on both the government and the LTTE.

In this endeavor the UTHR(J) would try to sustain a space for political pluralism, freedom of conscience, human rights accountability and civil society based activism with the support of many within and without the Tamil community. To sustain this network and continue our work, we solicit support from well-wishers

In the last decade we have worked with minimal costs and infrastructure. We have also tried when possible to avoid receiving funds from institutional donors.  In fact, the donations of many of you, and the volunteer efforts of several individuals have been essential to enabling our work in the past.

 Our ability to proceed with our current work, and expand into new projects, will depend on our financial resources and the volunteer efforts of our friends.   We have planned a few new initiatives for the coming year.  These include the following:

·        We would like to supplement our briefings and reports with regular news analysis on our website.  As the only website focused on the human rights situation in Sri Lanka, we would like to expand our coverage of current events from a human rights perspective.

·        We plan to make our website bilingual by having our work translated into Tamil. In the past some of our publications have been translated, but we would like to make all our future work available in both English and Tamil so that it will be accessible to the Tamil speaking audience in Sri Lanka and the diaspora.

·        We plan to translate ‘The Arrogance of Power’ into Tamil and serialize it on our website, and if possible, in Tamil newspapers and periodicals.

If you wish to support us, please send an e-mail to contact@uthr.org and we shall give you further infomation.

Thank You,

UTHR(J)

We support all efforts working for a sustainable and just peace in Sri Lanka. To this extent we see that peace, the democratization of political space, and a fair political solution are distinct but deeply intertwined goals. While different actors may focus their energies on one or the other of these goals, their efforts should be informed by the essential connection between these issues.
It is from this perspective that we advocate peace building in Sri Lanka. Peace is more than the management of conflict; peace should entail movement toward the sharing of political power through substantial devolution and the recognition of the rights of minorities. Peace is also more than the cessation of hostilities between different armies; peace should also involve the cessation of hostilities between the political authority (be it the Sri Lankan government or the LTTE) and the population it governs, particularly its minorities and dissidents.
So we should always ask, "what kind of peace?" Had Hitler conquered the world there would be a certain kind of peace between different armies for a temporary period - but not a peace that is sustainable or just. When faced with the hardships of war we may be tempted to opt for an illusory peace that is merely a temporary hiatus in a war that becomes even more ruthless and brings on even greater hardship. We hold little hope for a short-term "peace" imposed by the state via pure military hegemony without inter-ethnic justice and the decentralization of power, or temporary military arrangements that appease the LTTE to manage the conflict. In our struggle for greater democratization of political space, for respect for human rights and accountability by all political actors, we seek to build a lasting and just peace.
